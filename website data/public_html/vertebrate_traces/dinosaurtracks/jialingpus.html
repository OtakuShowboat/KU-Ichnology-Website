Jialingpus: Zhen et al., 1983

JUNIOR SYNONYM(S): N/A

DESCRIPTION: Medium–sized, tridactyl tracks with occasional manus and tail traces. Hallux (digit I) is rarely preserved. Divarication angle between digit II–IV ranges from 44 degrees to 52 degrees. Digit II’s pad I is larger than digit IV’s pads. The metatarsophalangeal region is closer to the proximal end of digit IV than to the proximal ends of digit II and III.

BEHAVIOR(S): Repichnia

ENVIRONMENTAL SETTINGS: Continental; terrestrial

POSSIBLE TRACEMAKERS: Dinosaur, likely a therapod

GEOLOGIC RANGE: Upper Jurassic–Lower Cretaceous

REMARKS: Emended by Xing et al., 2011

Taenidium : (Heer, 1877)

DESCRIPTION: Unlined or thinly-lined, cylindrical to subcylindrical straight to sinuous tubes with meniscate backfill and radiate downwards; commonly, oblique to bedding and tend not to cross-sect others, unless made by different organism.

BEHAVIOR(S): Feeding burrows of deposit-feeders.

ENVIRONMENTAL SETTINGS: Shallow to deep marine settings, continental terrestrial, fluvial.

POSSIBLE TRACEMAKERS: Annelid worms.

GEOLOGIC RANGE: Cambrian-recent.

ADDITIONAL REFERENCES: (Emended: D'Alessandro and Bromley, 1987)(Keighley and Pickerill, 1994)(Savrda et al., 2002)(Ekdale, et al., 2007).

REMARKS: Ichnogenus should be restricted to the morphologies of the type material (counter to Keighley and Pickerill, 1994). In eolian environments, Taenidium is thinly lined, meniscate burrows subparallel to the slipface and curve slightly upwards, downwards, and sideways in the sediment. Menisci are less regular in shape and spacing than in Entradichunus and no evidence of roof collapse in the burrow.

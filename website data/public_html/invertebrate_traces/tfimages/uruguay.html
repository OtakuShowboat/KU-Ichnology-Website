Uruguay : (Zuener and Manning, 1976)

DESCRIPTION: Ordered, nested capsules of Celliforma in a compact pattern. All cells share a common wall.

BEHAVIOR(S): Hold to house eggs before hatching. Cells may be filled with pollen, fungi, or angiosperms

ENVIRONMENTAL SETTINGS: Occur in a wide range of continental settings,and occur in all soil types with the exception of swamp-land. These traces tend to occur above the water table in both proximal and distal alluvial and lacustrine settings.

POSSIBLE TRACEMAKERS: Bees, bee-like insects, wasps (all colonial species).

GEOLOGIC RANGE: Triassic-recent.

ADDITIONAL REFERENCES: Genise & Bown, 1996

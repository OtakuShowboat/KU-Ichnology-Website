Celliforma: Brown, 1934

DESCRIPTION: Consists of capsule shaped, smooth cells. Some of these cells have remnants of the surrounding media still visible. They are flask shaped to varying degrees and may contain fossilized pollen and spores.

BEHAVIOR(S): Reproduction. Single cells to small clusters of cells created by solitary species. Grouped created by colonies.

ENVIRONMENTAL SETTINGS: Occur in a wide range of continental settings,and occur in all soil types with the exception of swamp-land. These traces tend to occur above the water table in both proximal and distal alluvial and lacustrine settings.

POSSIBLE TRACEMAKERS: Bees, bee-like insects, wasps

GEOLOGIC RANGE: Triassic-recent

ADDITIONAL REFERENCES: Hasiotis, et. al., 1995; Hasiotis, 2003, 2004, 2006

REMARKS: 3D anaglyphs of Celliforma can be found here. The geologic range indicates that these formed before the first known flowering plants.

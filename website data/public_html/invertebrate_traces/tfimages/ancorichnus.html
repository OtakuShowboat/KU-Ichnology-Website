Ancorichnus: Heinburg, 1974

DESCRIPTION: Tubular burrows, circular to elliptical in cross-section, consisting of relatively thick, unornamented wall linings surrounding well-developed, meniscate burrow fills. Distal ends of menisci may blend directly into the wall structure. Burrows are rarely branched and are predominantly horizontal or gently inclined to bedding. Distinctly lined, smooth-walled, cylindrical to subcylindrical meniscate burrows of variable orientation. Menisci extend to, or into, the wall lining.

BEHAVIOR(S): Deposit feeding, habitation.

ENVIRONMENTAL SETTINGS: Continental

POSSIBLE TRACEMAKERS: Polychaetes insect larvae.

GEOLOGIC RANGE: Jurassic-recent

ADDITIONAL REFERENCES: (Emended: Frey et al., 1984)

Palmichnium: Richter, 1954

DESCRIPTION: Trackway consisting of a median furrow flanked by paired sets of three imprints with concave adaxial with a curve posterior of the midlength of the imprints. Rarely, imprints are arranged parallel to track axis, appearing as two single rows of tracks. Depth of imprints decreases from outer imprints to inner imprints. Trackway has a constant transverse width of 79 cm.

BEHAVIOR(S): Repichnia

ENVIRONMENTAL SETTINGS: Shallow marine or freshwater environments

POSSIBLE TRACEMAKERS: Large arthropod with 6 legs such as eurypterid or scorpion

GEOLOGIC RANGE: Silurian- Mississippian

ADDITIONAL REFERENCES: Emended by Briggs and Rolfe (1983); Braddy and Milner (1998)

REMARKS: Description and geologic range from Briggs and Rolfe (1983) and Braddy and Milner (1998)

Ichnology of the Catskill Formation:
The trace fossil assemblage of the Upper Devonian Catskill Formation of north-central PA was investigated. The trace fossil associations of the different paleoenvironments (point bar, proximal and distal floodplains) recorded there will be assessed. The main objective of this research is to determine the role of soil biota in soil formation in the early evolution of terrestrial ecosystems. This linkage between tracemaking biota and soil formation is important because soil biota today is the driving force in the nutrient cycling system, and that they have been demonstrated to be ecosystem engineers that modulate the flow and recycling of nutrients. Research is on going.
Researchers: Jones and Hasiotis

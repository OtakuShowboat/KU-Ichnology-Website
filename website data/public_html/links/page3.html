Further Reading

If you are further interested in ichnology or are looking for other images, these are links to some other pages on ichnology. All links open in a new tab.

General Ichnology Links

http://www.envs.emory.edu/faculty/MARTIN/ichnology/
Dr. Anthony Martin's (Emory University) website. Contains overview information on ichnology and ichnofacies concepts, as well as a history of the field and a photo glossary.

http://oak.cats.ohiou.edu/~hembree/index.htm
The website of the Continental Ichnology Research Laboratory led by Dr. Dan Hembree. Focuses on ichnology in the continental realm and hosts numerous images of burrows and burrowing experiments.

http://research.eas.ualberta.ca/ichnology/
Ichnology Research Group website. Group led by Dr. George Pemberton from the University of Alberta. Lots of information on ichnology from the marine perspective.

http://www.ldeo.columbia.edu/~polsen/nbcp/tjfp.html
The Triassic-Jurassic Footprint Project. Information and papers about different trackways from the Mesozoic.

http://www.fossil-art.dk/frontpage-UK.html
The fossil art exhibit created by Dr. Adolf Seilacher and Hans Luginsland. Has links to many Trace Fossils.

http://www.skullsite.co.uk/prints/
Contains some basic information on ichnology, as well as pictures and comparisons of several types of modern tracks.

http://www.ldeo.columbia.edu/~polsen/nbcp/newarktrackbib.html
Bibliography for the ichnology of the Newark Supergroup.

http://openpaleo.blogspot.com/
The Open Source Paleontologist, a blog that reports current exciting news in paleontology. Often discusses ichnology, and has open access to some academic papers as well.

http://ncseweb.org/cej/5/1/mantracks-fossils-say-no
Article hosted by the National Center for Science Education, using ichnology to disprove creationist arguments.

Dinosaur Ichnology Links

http://palaeo.gly.bris.ac.uk/Palaeofiles/Tracks/default.html
Website hosted by the Biology and Biodiversity Research Group, hosted at the University of Bristol. Focuses on Dinosaur trackways.

http://digsfossils.com/fossils/footprints_main.html
Research website detailing dinosaur trackways from the Triassic and Jurassic of North America. Contains many pictures and some video. Contains some other links to information on ichnology at their homepage: http://digsfossils.com/

http://paleo.cc/paluxy/photogal.htm
Photo gallery of dinosaur tracks, updated regularly.

http://www.uky.edu/KGS/education/Mesozoic.htm
http://www.uky.edu/KGS/education/Mesozoic2.htm
http://www.uky.edu/KGS/education/dinotracks.htm
Collection of references about dinosaur trace fossils and trace fossils associated with dinosaurs.

Academic Resource Links

http://palaios.geoscienceworld.org/
Website for the Journal Palaios. Some free articles are available directly from the website.

http://www.sepm.org/jsr
Website for the Journal of Sedimentary Research.

KU Resource Links

http://ku.edu/
Homepage for the University of Kansas.

http://www.geo.ku.edu/
Homepage for the Department of Geology at KU.

http://www.paleo.ku.edu/
Homepage for the Paleontological Institute, which is housed at KU. The Paleo Institute publishes the Treatise on Invertebrate Paleontology, an important resource for the study of invertebrate fossils.

http://www.nhm.ku.edu/
Homepage for the KU Natural History Museum, which is part of the Department of Ecology and Evolutionary Biology.
